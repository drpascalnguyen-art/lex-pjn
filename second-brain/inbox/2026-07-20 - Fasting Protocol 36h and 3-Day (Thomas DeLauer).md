# Fasting Protocol — 36-Hour & 3-Day Fasts (based on Thomas DeLauer)

**Date:** 2026-07-20
**Goal:** Reduce body fat % with emphasis on visceral fat
**Sources:** Thomas DeLauer's recent YouTube content + the research he cites (links at bottom)

> ⚠️ **Safety first:** You're a healthcare professional, so you know the drill — extended fasting is not appropriate with diabetes/hypoglycemia, on glucose-lowering or blood-pressure meds, with a history of disordered eating, or while under high physical stress. Clear a 3-day fast with your own physician. Break the fast immediately if you get dizzy, heart palpitations, confusion, or feel genuinely unwell (not just hungry).

---

## Why this approach (DeLauer's latest key video)

His September 2025 video **"This Fasting Method Shrinks 33% More Visceral Fat than Any Other Diet"** is built on the Arciero 2023 study in *Obesity*: **intermittent fasting + protein pacing (IF-P)** beat calorie restriction at identical calories:

- **Visceral fat: −33% vs −14%**
- Total fat mass: −16% vs −9%
- Weight: −9% vs −5%

The studied protocol: **1–2 fasting periods of 36–60 hours per week**, with a small (~400 kcal) protein-based intake allowed on the fast day, and on eating days **4 protein-paced meals of 25–50 g protein each** (~35% protein / 35% carb / 30% fat). Takeaway for you: the 36-hour fast is the *workhorse* for visceral fat, the 3-day fast is the occasional deeper reset (autophagy, insulin sensitivity) — and **what you do on eating days (protein pacing) drives half the result.**

## PHASE 1 — Getting Ready (both fasts)

### 2–3 days out
- **Taper carbs down** (~<100 g/day, then <50 g the last day). Entering partially keto-adapted = far easier transition, less "keto flu" during the fast.
- **Protein-pace your meals**: 3–4 meals of 25–50 g protein. Preserves muscle going in.
- **Hydrate + start electrolytes early**: begin salting food generously; magnesium ~200–400 mg/day (glycinate at night).
- **Reduce caffeine slightly** if you're a heavy user, to avoid a withdrawal headache stacking on fast-day hunger.
- **Schedule it**: start the fast after dinner. Sleep covers the first 8–10 hours. For a 36-hour fast: dinner Mon 7pm → eat Wed 7am. For 3 days: dinner Sun 7pm → break Wed 7pm.

### The last meal before the fast
DeLauer's specific guidance:
- **Keep it modest, not a "last supper" binge** — a smaller meal lets the stomach down-regulate sooner (less hunger later, less urge to over-eat when refeeding).
- **Build it around protein + omega-3/MCT fats**: salmon is his go-to (DHA supports cell membranes through the fast), or lean protein + fish oil + a little MCT/coconut oil.
- **Low carb, low-ish fiber**, non-starchy veg only — you want stable blood sugar going into hour 12.

## PHASE 2 — During the Fast

### What to drink (both fasts)
- **Water**: 2.5–3.5 L/day. Fasting is a diuretic — you lose water AND minerals.
- **Electrolytes daily** (the #1 reason people feel awful and quit):
  - Sodium: **3–5 g** (spread through the day; pinch of sea salt in water, or an unsweetened electrolyte mix — DeLauer uses Redmond Re-Lyte)
  - Potassium: **1–2 g**
  - Magnesium: **300–400 mg**
- **Black coffee** (1–3 cups, mornings) — blunts hunger, supports fat mobilization.
- **Green tea / matcha** — catechins + theanine; his favorite fasting beverage for fat oxidation with calm energy.
- **Apple cider vinegar** in water (1 tbsp) — helps blood sugar stability and hunger waves.
- **Nothing sweet** — including most zero-cal sweeteners. Keep the palate quiet.

### Movement & training
- **Walk a lot.** This is DeLauer's biggest lever — 10–20k steps/day. Low-intensity movement drains glycogen and pushes fat (including visceral fat) into circulation for fuel without spiking cortisol.
- **Light resistance training is fine and useful** (especially ~hours 16–24) — keeps muscle stimulated.
- **No HIIT or heavy max-effort work**, especially on day 2–3 of the long fast. Cortisol + no fuel = muscle loss and misery.
- Sauna/cold exposure fine if already adapted; be extra careful about hydration.

### 36-hour fast — hour-by-hour shape
| Hours | What's happening | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| 0–12 (overnight) | Glycogen burning down | Sleep, water on waking |
| 12–18 | Switching to fat/ketones; hunger waves | Coffee/green tea, morning walk, busy work block |
| 18–24 | Fat oxidation ramps, hunger usually *drops* | Best window for a walk + light lifting; electrolytes |
| 24–36 | Ketones high, autophagy ramping, growth hormone up | Evening: ACV + salt water, early bed. Wake and break fast at hr 36 |

*Optional (study-consistent):* if the fast feels brutal, the Arciero protocol allowed **~400 kcal of protein** (e.g., a casein/whey shake) in the evening of the fast day — it still out-performed dieting. A pure water/electrolyte fast is the cleaner default; use the protein option as a fallback rather than the plan.

### 3-day (72-hour) fast — day-by-day
- **Day 1 (0–24 h):** Same as above. Easiest day. Normal work, long walk.
- **Day 2 (24–48 h):** The hard day — hunger hormones (ghrelin) peak around 24–36 h, then decline. Double down on electrolytes (this is when headaches/fatigue hit if you skimp on sodium). Light activity only. Expect poor sleep; magnesium at night helps.
- **Day 3 (48–72 h):** Usually surprisingly good — deep ketosis, mental clarity, hunger low. Autophagy and insulin-sensitivity benefits are peaking. Keep intensity low, stay warm (feeling cold is normal), plan the refeed.
- **Bone broth option:** for a first 3-day fast, DeLauer is fine with **1 cup of bone broth once or twice a day** — technically a "modified fast," slightly less autophagy, massively better adherence and electrolytes. Recommended for round one; go pure water + electrolytes on later rounds.

## PHASE 3 — Breaking the Fast (the part most people botch)

**Cardinal rules (DeLauer):** break with **lean protein**, keep the first meals **low-carb and low-fat-ish**, **no carb+fat combos**, no raw fibrous veg, and go *small* — your stomach has shrunk and insulin sensitivity is sky-high (great for nutrient partitioning, bad if you refeed with pizza).

### Breaking the 36-hour fast
1. **First food:** 4–6 oz lean protein (chicken breast, white fish, egg whites, or a whey/bone-broth protein) OR 1 cup bone broth.
2. **Wait 60 minutes.**
3. **Meal 2:** normal-sized meal — protein + healthy fats + cooked vegetables. Still easy on dense carbs.
4. **Rest of the day:** return to protein-paced eating (25–50 g protein × 3–4 meals). This is a *great* day to strength train after meal 2 — nutrients go to muscle.

### Breaking the 3-day fast (slower — take ~24 h to fully re-ramp)
1. **Hour 0:** 1 cup bone broth with salt. Wait 30 min, assess. Second cup if all good.
2. **Hour 1–2:** 4–8 oz lean protein (white fish is ideal — easy to digest, DHA). No sides yet.
3. **Hour 3–4:** small meal — protein + cooked veg + a little fat (avocado/olive oil).
4. **Evening:** normal protein + fat + veg dinner. Keep starches minimal today.
5. **Day +1:** resume full protein-paced eating; reintroduce starchy carbs around training. Avoid alcohol for 48 h.
6. **Watch for** (mainly after 3+ days): fatigue, swelling, muscle cramps after eating = refeeding-syndrome warning signs → slow down, prioritize electrolytes (especially phosphorus/potassium — eggs, fish help).

## Putting it together — weekly rhythm for visceral fat

- **Workhorse:** one 36-hour fast per week (e.g., Mon dinner → Wed breakfast).
- **Eating days:** protein pacing — 4 meals, 25–50 g protein each, ~35/35/30 P/C/F. This matched the study that produced the −33% visceral fat result.
- **Deep reset:** one 3-day fast per quarter (or monthly at most). Not a weekly tool.
- **Daily:** 10k+ steps, resistance training 3–4×/week on eating days, sleep 7+ h.
- **Track:** waist circumference weekly (best cheap visceral-fat proxy), not just scale weight. DEXA every ~3 months if you want the real visceral number.
